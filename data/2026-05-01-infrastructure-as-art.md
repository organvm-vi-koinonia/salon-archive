## Theme

Can institutional infrastructure be a creative medium? When does system design become art? This salon explores the boundary between engineering and aesthetics — examining cases where the container is as interesting as the content.

## Readings (pre-session)

Participants should read at least 2 of the following before attending:

1. **Shannon Mattern** — "Maintenance and Care" (Places Journal, 2018). On the invisible labor of maintaining systems.
2. **Keller Easterling** — "Extrastatecraft: The Power of Infrastructure Space" (2014), Chapter 1. Infrastructure as a medium of information.
3. **RFC 2549** — "IP over Avian Carriers with Quality of Service" (1999). Technical writing as comedy as art.
4. **Stewart Brand** — "How Buildings Learn" (1994), Chapter 2: "Shearing Layers." How buildings adapt through time as systems of nested rates.
5. **Rem Koolhaas** — "Junkspace" (2002). On the aesthetics of contemporary architectural infrastructure.

## Discussion Prompts

1. When the ORGANVM governance-rules.json codifies natural law (LEX-I through LEX-XI), is that documentation or philosophy? Does the formal structure of the governance make it art?
2. Is there a point where system complexity becomes aesthetically valuable in itself — or is complexity always a failure of compression (LIM-III)?
3. The organ-aesthetic.yaml files define visual identity per organ. How does aesthetic governance differ from aesthetic creation?
