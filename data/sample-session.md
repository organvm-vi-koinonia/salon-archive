## Notes

Exploring how self-referential structures in ORGAN-I (recursive-engine, radix-recursiva) can be understood as creative acts rather than purely formal constructs. The recursive-engine's generative entity model treats recursion not as mere repetition but as a process that produces novelty at each level — much like a musical theme that transforms through variations. We examine Hofstadter's strange loops, Bateson's levels of learning, and how the organvm system itself is a recursive structure (organs that describe organs).
